About Me

I am a Registered Occupational Therapist working with acute medicine clients, and with clients involved in motor vehicle accidents in the private sector.

I developed a passion for occupational therapy when I was introduced to the profession as a volunteer for 5 years in the programs at Holland Bloorview Kids Rehabilitation Hospital while completing my undergraduate degree at the University of Toronto. I then completed my Master of Science in Occupational Therapy degree at McMaster University.

My interests within the field include neurological rehabilitation, pediatrics, and promoting the health and well-being of individuals with disabilities. Extra training I have taken includes Brain Fx certification, non-violent crisis intervention training, QPR suicide prevention, and gentle persuasive approaches (GPA) in dementia care. I have also taken recent workshops in concussion management, motivational interviewing, practical intervention strategies for anxiety disorders, Handwriting Without Tears, and sensory-based interventions for children.

I am eager to continue to develop my skills in interprofessional collaboration, client-centered practice, and disability advocacy.
